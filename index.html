Why SIEM migrations fail and how to break the cycle | Cribl

SIEM migrations frequently fail due to organizational, contractual, and structural issues – not the technology itself.

  • Vendor lock-in is common via proprietary data collection, retention formats, and restrictive contracts with auto-renewal clauses and data egress fees.
  • Teams often lack experience migrating SIEMs and struggle to quickly gain proficiency in new platforms while maintaining existing security coverage.
  • Security programs often become overly dependent on the SIEM vendor, tying detection logic and workflows to a specific platform.
  • Migrations routinely take longer than planned due to underestimated effort, competing priorities and the need to rebuild (not migrate) content/rules.

Why agentic analytics breaks the modern data stack — and how OcientAIQ™ is built for what's next

OcientAIQ™ is designed to address the limitations of the modern data stack when used with agentic analytics.

  • Agentic analytics introduces a “modal tax” – penalties in tokens, accuracy, and latency – when agents must move between different data systems (e.g., Snowflake, SageMaker).
  • OcientAIQ™ aims to eliminate this tax by providing a unified data platform with native support for SQL, machine learning, geospatial data, and real-time analytics within a single engine.
  • The platform supports agent-created databases and materialization (scratch tables) across modalities without requiring data movement or external lookups.
  • OcientAIQ™’s pricing is fixed per-core and capacity-based, removing cost constraints on agent exploration and iteration; further details are available at docs.ocient.com.

The federal cybersecurity logging mandate just changed — and it's good news for everyone defending critical infrastructure

OcientAIQ™ Unified Data Platform is positioned to meet the new federal cybersecurity logging mandate (M-26-14), which shifts focus from log retention to the searchability and operational usability of logs for both Continuous Event Monitoring (CEM) and Threat Hunting, Investigation, Response, and Forensics (THIRF).

  • OcientAIQ™ reduces SIEM ingestion volume (by 40-60%) through pre-SIEM enrichment – deduplication, behavioral scoring, and threat context – improving alert quality and reducing costs.
  • The platform stores full-fidelity telemetry for 12-24 months, enabling sub-ten-second queries across large datasets (e.g., 50 billion records) using ANSI SQL, without data movement delays.
  • Ocient’s architecture supports the mandate’s required data retention tiers, keeping the entire retained corpus searchable, meeting "Advanced" and "Optimal" maturity model requirements.
  • The platform includes an interface compatible with Large Language Models (LLMs) via Model Context Protocol (MCP), allowing AI agents to proactively hunt threats and reconstruct attack timelines across the retained historical data.

The hidden infrastructure challenge of agentic AI

Ocient addresses the increased infrastructure strain caused by agentic AI systems, which generate significantly more queries than traditional analyst workflows.

  • The OcientAIQ™ platform aims to provide a unified data platform for AI, avoiding the need to move data across fragmented systems.
  • The core claim is that agentic AI increases query volume, not just complexity, often exceeding the capacity of existing analytical infrastructure.
  • Ocient advocates for consolidating analytical capabilities and applying governance at machine speed to reduce friction and cost.
  • The platform focuses on making system costs and resource contention visible, and treating agent workloads as production workloads.
